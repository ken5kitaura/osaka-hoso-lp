### [section #proof] We proved it on ourselves.
- **eyebrow**: Proof

Proof

Our first test subject was ourselves. Radio Osaka (OBC) — a broadcaster we invested in, on the air since 1958, built on decades of analog processes. We didn't observe from the outside. We walked in as members of the team and rebuilt it with our own hands — from accounting to programming.

Start from the hardest case. Prove it there first. That's the only credential that counts.

Paper slips, manual tallying, accounting that lived inside one person's head.

AI-powered automatic journaling and analysis. Management numbers visible in real time.

This is what happened when a paper-and-stamp broadcaster met AI.

2,500–3,000 entries / month

processed automatically by AI.

- Every paper voucher and every approval stamp — eliminated. Purchase orders, transfers, invoices, expenses — all digitized.

- The endless chain of "write on paper → copy → enter → tally on paper again → re-enter in Excel" — ripped out entirely.

- Exception handling that existed only in veterans' heads — untangled. Multiple paths for the same process — unified. Now anyone can run the books and get the same result.

#### AI beyond accounting: Daily Radio Osaka

Every topic from the day's live broadcast, automatically condensed by AI into a 20-minute audio summary, delivered daily. Radio Osaka's character Benten Obi has been appointed as an AI announcer. AI isn't just working behind the scenes — it's reaching listeners directly.

Created in partnership with PLUS IMPACT Inc.

We don't deal in theory.

We proved it ourselves. That's why we can deliver it with confidence.

History

From our founding in 1945 to our rebirth in 2026.

Founded as Maruichi Shokai (Founder: Gentaro Kato).

December 17 — incorporated as Maruichi Kosan Co., Ltd.

Succession: Katsuhiro Kato (1979) / Masataka Kato (2003) / Yorihiko Kato (2017).

Headquarters relocated to Tsuneyoshi, Konohana-ku.

Invested in Radio Osaka (OBC). Yorihiko Kato appointed Representative Director and Chairman of Radio Osaka.

### [section #representatives] Why now.
- **eyebrow**: Message

Message

One man built this company. Eighty years later, it's in the hands of two. One carries the legacy. The other rewrites the playbook.

#### Yorihiko Kato

Representative Director (concurrently Chairman, Radio Osaka)

Born 1967, raised in Osaka. Started his first venture while still a student at Kwansei Gakuin University and founded the advertising company Nikko (now GMO NIKKO) in 1992. In 2008, he sold the company to GMO Internet Group and moved to Singapore. From there, he operated as an angel investor across Asia, backing more than 80 Japanese startups. In 2017, he took the helm of Maruichi — the 80-year-old family enterprise — as its fourth-generation leader.

Kato knows both sides: the weight of a legacy business and the edge of building something new. He chose to go all in on Radio Osaka — a broadcaster the company invested in — not from a distance, but up close. Not "invest and watch." Full commitment, personally on the ground. To make that commitment unmistakable — inside and out — he changed the 80-year-old family company's name to Osaka Hoso. For Radio Osaka's next chapter. Everything the company has. Everything he has.

> He changed the company name to make one thing clear: we're in this together, all the way forward.

That commitment isn't just words. Under Kato's direction, concrete AI services are already live — from internal automation to Daily Radio Osaka, where AI summarizes the day's broadcasts (⑤ Proof).

Yorihiko Kato's columns and articles: machikado-creative.jp ↗

#### Kengo Kitaura

Director (Management & Administration Division, Radio Osaka)

Born in Osaka. Left for the U.S. right after middle school. After returning to Japan and running businesses, he moved to Cambodia in 2009. In 2014, he founded AGRIBUDDY, an agri-fintech company, and served as CEO. He built a system to collect and visualize farming data from smallholder farmers who had no records and no credit history, connecting them to financial services. Awards include Grand Prize at the Nikkei FinTech Conference and Global Startup Award "Founder of the Year" (the first ASEAN/Cambodian company to win). He returned to Japan in 2023 and now works on automating corporate accounting and back-office operations with AI.

Kitaura is not a commentator. He's an operator who has made things work in the places furthest from digital. In Cambodian villages, he connected farmers with no records and no credit to data, then to finance. The older, more paper-heavy, more manual an organization is, the more dramatically technology can transform it — that conviction was forged in the field, not at a desk. Now Kitaura aims that same force at old organizations in Japan. The AI transformation of Radio Osaka — from operations to accounting — he designed and built it himself.

> The tools are available to everyone. The difference is knowing what to aim them at. Reverse-engineer from the outcome. Redesign from there.
