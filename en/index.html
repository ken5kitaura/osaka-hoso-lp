About Us

Eighty years. All in Osaka.

Since 1945, Osaka Hoso has been rooted in Osaka for eighty years. The business has changed shape with the times — but one thing never has: trust. Today we're building on that trust to take on a new line of work — transforming companies with AI.

Our Business

The older the company, the more AI changes it.

People assume AI is for young, digital-native companies. We think the opposite. The organizations with the most to gain are the old ones — buried in paper, run on manual labor, doing things the same way for decades. We redesign a company's entire back office — accounting, finance, and beyond — with AI.

01

Start from the decision, not the tool

We design backward from the question that actually matters: what numbers do you need to run the business? Then we rebuild everything down to daily data entry. Design first. Tools second.

02

AI is an amplifier

The real skill is seeing what to hand AI in the first place. We don't drop a tool in your lap and leave — we hand over the whole working system, the kind that actually produces results.

03

Ridiculous speed

From idea to working system, faster than should be possible — because we run on AI ourselves.

Yorihiko Kato

Representative Director (concurrently Chairman & Representative Director, Radio Osaka)

Born 1967, raised in Osaka. He threw himself into entrepreneurship while still at Kwansei Gakuin University, founding the ad agency Nikko (now GMO NIKKO) in 1992. In 2008 he sold the company to GMO Internet Group and moved his base to Singapore. From there he worked across Asia as an angel investor, backing more than 80 Japanese startups. In 2017 he took over the 80-year-old family business — the Maruichi group — as its head.

Someone who knows both the weight of a family business and the front line of building something new. Kato chose to take Radio Osaka's future seriously — really seriously. Not the invest-and-leave-the-rest-to-others kind of distance. The company would back the station with everything it had, and he would step onto the floor himself, as one of the team. To make that stance unmistakable — inside the company and out — he renamed the 80-year-old family business "Osaka Hoso." For Radio Osaka's future. The company, and the man, all in.

I changed our name to make it unmistakable — we're in this, moving forward, together.

And it's more than words. Under Kato's lead, concrete AI services keep launching — from automating the back office to Daily Radio Osaka, the show AI summarizes every day. (See Proof.)

Now
Actually get it done

Kengo Kitaura

Director (Management Administration, Radio Osaka)

Born in Osaka. He left for the U.S. the moment he finished junior high. After running businesses back in Japan, he moved to Cambodia in 2009. In 2014 he founded the agri-fintech company AGRIBUDDY and served as CEO — building a system that gathered and visualized farming data from smallholders with no records and no credit history, and connected them to finance. The work won top prize at the Nikkei FinTech Conference and "Founder of the Year" at the Global Startup Awards (a first for an ASEAN / Cambodian company). He returned to Japan in 2023 and now works on automating companies' accounting and back-office operations with AI.

Kitaura is no commentator. He's a builder who has taken the world furthest from digital and made it work — with his own hands. In the villages of Cambodia, he connected farmers with no records and no credit to data, and to finance. The older, more paper-bound, more manual an organization is, the more technology can change it — a conviction he earned in the field, not on paper. Now he's aiming that same force at Japan's old organizations. Radio Osaka's AI transformation — a station Osaka Hoso invested in — he designed and built himself, from the books to the floor.

Anyone can get the tools. The difference is seeing what to make them do. Work backward from the outcome, and redesign from there.
